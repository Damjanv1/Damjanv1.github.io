Employment & Pay

Employment figures are from ABS Labour Force Detailed (Feb 2025), aggregated to ANZSCO 3-digit minor groups and rounded to the nearest 5,000. Treat them as order-of-magnitude estimates — the ABS releases these quarterly and the figures shift. Pay data is from ABS Employee Earnings 2023. Job growth projections are from Jobs and Skills Australia (May 2025 to May 2035), covering ~350 ANZSCO occupations.

AI Exposure Scores

This is the part that needs the most caveats. The 2021 scores adapt Felten, Raj & Seamans' AIOE index, built before large language models became mainstream. The 2023 scores use Eloundou et al. "GPTs are GPTs" (Science, 2023), crosswalked from US SOC codes via ISCO-08 to ANZSCO — that crosswalk introduces noise. The 2024 scores draw on Gmyrek's ILO analysis, coded natively in ISCO-08. The 2026 scores apply the scoring methodology from Andrej Karpathy's jobs.karpathy.ai to ANZSCO descriptions. None of these were designed for Australian occupations. The relative positions and directional trends are the useful signal — individual decimal points are not.

How AI Exposure Changed by Sector (2021 → 2026)

Weighted-average exposure per ANZSCO major group across four studies. The 2021→2023 gap is the GPT-4 moment — white-collar professional work crossed into high-risk territory in under two years.

Key Findings

Biggest jump

Clerical & Admin

Highest-exposed sector throughout all four studies. Contact centre, data entry, and accounting clerk roles are already contracting — AI is accelerating a trend that predates LLMs.

Surprise riser

Professionals

The 2021→2023 jump was largest here. Lawyers, accountants, analysts — work that looked safe in the pre-ChatGPT era moved firmly into high-exposure territory once GPT-4 arrived.

Most insulated

Trades & Labourers

Electricians, plumbers, carpenters — flat across all four studies. Physical work in variable environments has a natural ceiling on AI impact. Growing infrastructure demand adds further insulation.

Nuanced

ICT Professionals

Very high exposure but also the fastest-growing sector. AI is restructuring the work, not eliminating it — productivity per developer is rising, and demand keeps pace.

Caveat

On the scores

2021–2024 scores crosswalk published academic studies to ANZSCO. 2026 scores use the Karpathy LLM-scoring methodology. The crosswalks introduce noise — use the trends, not the decimals.

The Four Quadrants

Top-left

Safe Havens

Low AI exposure, positive job growth. Physical trades, healthcare support, construction. Strong demand, limited displacement risk. Best near-term employment outlook.

Top-right

Augmented

High exposure but growing. ICT, engineering, health diagnostics. AI restructures these roles — output per worker grows, but so does headcount (just more slowly than output).

Bottom-right

Vulnerable

High AI exposure and flat or declining jobs. Clerical, data entry, contact centres. These are already contracting. Slide to 2026 and watch the cluster grow.

Bottom-left

Fading

Low AI exposure but low or negative growth. Manufacturing, some agriculture. Structural decline from offshoring and conventional automation — not primarily an AI story.
