How AI Exposure Changed by Sector (2021 → 2026)

Weighted-average AI exposure score per ANZSCO major group across four independent studies. The 2021→2023 jump reflects GPT-4's arrival — white-collar professional work suddenly entered high-risk territory.

Key Findings

Biggest jump

Clerical & Admin

Highest-exposed sector throughout. Contact centre, data entry, and accounting clerks face structural workforce contraction that is already underway.

Surprise riser

Professionals

The 2021→2023 gap was the largest for this sector. GPT-4-level LLMs pushed lawyers, accountants, and analysts from "moderate" to "high" exposure in under two years.

Most insulated

Trades & Labourers

Physical trades (electricians, plumbers, carpenters) and outdoor labourers show minimal exposure change across all four studies. Growing infrastructure demand adds further insulation.

Nuanced

ICT Professionals

Very high exposure but also the fastest-growing sector. AI augments rather than eliminates — productivity per developer is soaring, but the occupation is not shrinking.

Context

About the scores

2021–2024 scores are crosswalked from published academic studies (Felten, Eloundou, Gmyrek). 2026 scores use the Karpathy LLM-scoring methodology applied to ANZSCO descriptions.

Risk vs Growth: Where Do Australian Occupations Sit?

Each circle is an ANZSCO occupation group — sized by employment, coloured by sector. X axis: AI exposure in 2026. Y axis: projected 5-year job growth. Quadrant lines at exposure = 5 and growth = 0%.

The Four Quadrants

Top-left

Safe Havens

Low AI exposure, positive job growth. Physical trades, healthcare support, construction, and childcare. Strong demand, limited AI displacement. Best near-term employment outlook.

Top-right

Augmented

High exposure but growing. ICT professionals, engineering, health diagnostics. AI is restructuring these roles — productivity jumps, headcount grows but at a slower rate than output.

Bottom-right

Vulnerable

High AI exposure and stagnant or declining jobs. Clerical, data entry, contact centres, accounting clerks. These roles are already contracting and AI accelerates the decline.

Bottom-left

Fading

Low AI exposure but also low/negative growth. Manual manufacturing, agriculture, some hospitality. Decline is structural (offshoring, automation) rather than AI-specific.

Policy note

The Clerk Problem

Australia has ~500k general clerks, data entry workers, and contact centre staff in the Vulnerable quadrant — the largest concentration of workers facing near-term AI displacement.
